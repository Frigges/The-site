Aeri

The aeri are a people of caravaneers found troughout the lands west of the great steppe, traveling from town to town as merchants, actors, and musicians. Despite looking a lot like humans, aeri are descended from a race of lesser celestials who survived the fall of heaven. This celestial heritage manifests itself primarily in the aeri's hair and eyes, as well as in the magical abilities they all inherently possess. Aeric hair colors are always bright, ranging from brown to white, and has a metallic luster to it. Aeri with hair that appears to be made from actual gold or silver are not uncommon. Their eyes come in all colors of the rainbow and then some, and sparkle like gemstones in a way human eyes could only hope to.

Fallen from Heaven

According to their own traditions, the aeri's progenitors, the cherubs, were once the watchers of the world, ready to come down from the heavenly plane to defend it from any cosmic threat stemming from without or within. However, like all other forces of order and good, they were powerless to stop the rise of Arenquis and the destruction of Nendor. This demonstrated their weakness to the titans, who would spend the next few millennia trying to find a way to enter the cherub's realm.

The titans were mysterious beings of primordial evil known to the cherubs only by their titles. Exactly how many there were is a matter of some debate among scholars and storytellers, but the general consensus is that they numbered somewhere between 9 and 12. Their ranks included such beings as The Burning One, The Silent Claws, and The Eraser, among others. Their wrath was terrible, and utterly destroyed the heavens. The few cherubs who survived were forever changed by the catastrophe, which came to be known as the fall of heaven. Prior to the fall, the cherubs had been winged and immortal, but friction from the fall burned their wings off, and the material world's lack of pervasive divine magic stripped them of their immortality.

From the Ashes

Cast out into the world, suddenly stripped of their immortality, the cherubs found themselves drifting from place to place, always without purpose or a place to call home. However, in this dark time, they were gathered together by a wandering warrior named Airinos. This warrior had come across one of the fallen cherubs, a woman named Brianna, on his journeys, and resolved to pull her out of her despair by showing her the beauty he saw everywhere around him. He succeeded, and the two married, vowing to travel the world together for the rest of their lives.

As the two taveled, news of their success and happiness reached other cherubs, and they were soon joined by many who wished to accompany them. Eventually the caravan grew too big, and split up. Many of the cherubs also took human partners who were drawn to the traveing lifestyle. Thus the celestial blood of the cherubs was diluted, and the aeri were born.

Aeric Religion

Aeri have no unified pantheon, instead typically choosing to adopt the gods of places they visit that appeal to them. Still, all of them pay respects to their founders—the wagon-parents—with some worshiping them as minor gods. According to aeric tradition, Airinos and Brianna are still out there in the world somewhere, forever traveling on their quest to see all the beautiful things the world has to offer.

Airinos

Brianna

Airinos is a cheerful and curious soul, seeing the beauty in everything and good in everyone. He is an unparalleled swordsman, but he uses his great skill to spare those he has to fight, and will only kill if it is absolutely necessary. He teaches the aeri to be ever curious and to push forward into unknown territory. He also teaches them to share what they can with those in need, especially other travelers, and to never deny those who would travel with them. The last rule is absolute, even if said person has commited crimes against the caravan before. The aeri are under no compulsion to trust the individual, but they must always share the joy of travel and exploration with those who desire it. Airinos believes that everyone can be redeemed if they experience the joy of traveling and seeing the wonders of the world.

Where Airinos is the driving force behind the aeri's journeys, Brianna is the loyalty and wisdom that keeps their caravans together. She teaches them to keep their wagons in good condition, to treat their draft animals well, and to trust and believe in their kin. She also teaches the aeri to not trust outsiders, and not let themselves be exploited. Aeri share with those in need, but are adamant about payment for their services, and are very protective about their secrets of music and swordplay.

Artists and Merchants

Since they do not stay in one place for very long, aeri naturally don't practice agriculture, nor do they practice other arts that require a permanent residence, such as blacksmithing. Aside from the obligatory basic horse handling, wagon care, and wilderness survival—which is learned by everyone—aeri women learn to sow and knit, and men learn to cook. Most of them also learn to use a sword, and to play one or more instruments and how to sing. The aeric style of music is very distinct and typically very popular in the places they visit, causing aeri musicians to be in high demand for special events such as weddings.

Every caravan is also home to at least one merchant, although typically a lot more. Despite being encouraged by their wagon-parents to travel far and wide, a majority of aeri caravans follow relatively set routes and return to a given place with reliable frequency, which is typically a good business oppurtunity both for the craftsmen, hunters, and farmers of the visited town, and for the aeri merchants and musicians.

Sanctioned by the Empire

Airinos is acknowledged and worshipped as a god of roads in the empire, and aeri are regarded as fully human under imperial law. Thus, aeri enjoy a unique position as the only non-human race to have full rights within the empire's borders—of course provided they swear fealty to the emperor and acknowledge him as the god above all, just as humans have to. All aeri who travel through the empire at some point at least pay lip service to the emperor, and some who travel their routes almost entirely within the empire even become full-blown loyalists—or as close to it as they can be while still traveling around in their wagons. On the other hand, many aeri outside of the empire are appalled by the idea, and some even believe the emperor to be an avatar of one of their old enemies; The Burning One.

Aeric Names

Aeri are usually named as their cherub ancestors were, but it is also very common for them to bear names after the regions they travel through. Go for a Jewish, Aramaic, or otherwise biblical name if you want the former.

Elves in Arkanos

As an elf in Arkanos you're one of a people in diaspora. Early Arkanian rulers tried to resettle the elves of Soriel around their land in an effort to integrate them, but because of the long lifespans of elves this didn't end up working out too well. Elves in current-day Arkanos live in small communities around the nation, grumbling over the excess of their mage overlords. As an elf in this enviroment you're likely to be viewed with some distrust by the Humans, but outright discrimination is rare, except for on the border with the empire. Your peers also generally distrust humans in kind, pointing to people such as the spellweaver and the emperor as examples of why humans will one day destroy the world. Also, given your lifespan, your grandparents or great grandparents would've been alive when Soriel was still the great enemy of Arkanos, which probably influenced you somehow.
Arkanian elves tend to be high elves. It's not impossible for the odd snow or gold elf to show up there, but they are faraway travelers and a rare sight indeed.

Elf Traits

Your elf character has the following racial traits.
   Age: Elves age about half as fast as humans until they reach maturity at around age 35. After that their aging slows down significantly. An elf at 500 is roughly as physically aged as a human at 80
   Size: Your size is Medium. Your specific height and build depends on your subrace, however, see below. Elf females are a little shorter than males on average, though the difference is less pronounced than in humans.
   Speed: Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
   Weavesense: You know the Detect Magic spell and can cast it once without using a spell slot. You can't do so again until you finish a short or long rest. Furthermore, when a spell takes effect anywhere within 30 feet of you, you immediately learn the spell's point of origin, level, and school of magic.
   Languages: You can speak, read, and write your local dialect of Nendorian, and one additional language of your choice. Nendorian is the melodic ancient language of the elves. Its dialects are Negalian, Sorieli, Héri, and Sirlam. The dialects are mostly mutually intelligible, but some words and expressions are bound to cause confusion.
   Subrace: The elven diaspora and holdout states have developed into a variety of subraces, spread out across the world.

High Elf
The high elves were the people of Soriel, the holdout state with the closest link and highest number of refugees from ancient Nendor. Soriel has since been destroyed, leaving the High Elves, once the most numerous of elvenkind, to live in minority communities in human lands. High elves tend to have ligher skin, comparable to that of mediterranean humans, and black, brown, or red hair, sometimes shifting towards a dark blue or olive brown.
   Size: High elves are half a hand taller than humans on average, but are more lightly built.
   Cantrip: You know one cantrip of your choice from the wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it.
   Heirs of Nendor: If you would fail a saving throw against a spell or magical effect, you can choose to succeed instead. You can use this trait a number of times equal to half your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
   Language: Your local dialect is Sorieli.

Snow Elf
The snow elf king is the leader of the great Negalian union to the north, and his people are usually the leaders whenever the peoples of Negal need to come together. Originally a refuge for dissenters and criminals, the snow elves are typically less arrogant and rowdier than other elves. Snow elves are pale, almost ghostly white, and their hair is rarely darker than a light brown.
   Size: Snow elves average about 2m. They are also fairly comparable to humans in build, on average only being slightly lighter.
   Lead by Example: You are a paragon, a leader of people, and your presence inspires others to follow. Whenever you make an attack roll against a creature, or force a creature to make a saving throw, your allies gain a +1 to attack rolls against that creature until the start of your next turn.
   Legalistic Heritage: You have proficiency in the history skill.
   Officer's Discipline: You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened. Whenever you succeed on a saving throw against being frightened, all allies within 60ft of you that are frightened can roll a saving throw against that condition, ending the effect on themselves on a success.
   Snow Elf Resistances: The endless war seasons have adapted their participants to the cold weather and spread it to their descendants. You have resistance to cold damage.
   Language: Your local dialect is Negali.

Gold Elf
Gold elves aren't very well developed lorewise, but are from an empire in [WEST_AFRICA] that mines ridiculous amounts of gold and has a strict social hierarchy based on wether you are a human (slave), half-elf (citizen), or full on elf (Master). They call themselves the "Hére", Nendorian for "Masters". Gold elf skin ranges from a very dark nut brown to pitch black, and their hair has bright metallic colors, such as gold, silver, copper, or brass
   Size: Gold elves are built like humans, but are taller, averaging 190cm.
   Saving Face: Gold elves must never show weakness in front of their allies, for fear of losing status. If you miss with an attack roll or fail an ability check or a saving throw, you can gain a bonus to the roll equal to the number of allies you can see within 30 feet of you (maximum bonus of +3). You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
   Gold Elf Proficiencies: You have proficiency with the scimitar, whip, longbow, and net. You also gain proficency with one musical instrument or gaming set of your choice.
   Language: Your local dialect is Héri.

Jungle Elf
Jungle elves are even less developed and unlikely to come up, since they are isolated from the rest of elvenkind by the uncrossable dragonsea.    Size: Jungle elves are about as tall as humans, and more lightly built.
   Jungle Walker: You can move through difficult terrain made out of plants without expending extra movement, and you gain a climbing speed of 20ft.

   Jungle Elf Proficiencies: You have proficiency with the shortsword, dagger, shortbow, and javelin. You also have proficency with the poisoner's kit.

Fiendspawn

Fiendspawn are born underground in the vast empire of the Lich King, often to unwilling mothers, be that woman mortal or fiend. In general, they are part of an attempt by the Lich King to create super soldiers for his war against the lowest plane, but they are also evidence of his activities underground, which are kept very secret as to not ever force him to spread his forces too thin for any reason.
To the world at large, you're not supposed to even exist. Any trace of fiend in you is to be carefully hidden, lest you would be persecuted by the common people, a target for forceful study by the order of binding or any local magic authority, and of course targeted and hunted relentlessly by agents of the Lich King eager to cover up their mess.

Kamrōmä

As a kamrōmä you hail from Komāt, the black land (Basically ancient egypt). You are a kind of human with the ears, eyes, and tail of a cat. Your feline features are a result of a blessing bestowed upon your kamrōmä or human parents by the regional goddess of childbirth and good harvests. This blessing also makes sure that you are one of at the very least two siblings, although often many more—children of blessed parents are always born as twins or triplets. Your native culture is fairly collectivist and focused on the good of the society above the individual. (You are also legally considered as one with your twin or two triplet siblings. more on that at some point). Kamrōmä skin tends to be dark brown, but the odd lighter shade from immigrant parents exists. Their body hair is soft like a cat's fur, and the hair on their head takes on feline colors and patterns, leading to quite colorful hair. Especially on women, who can display a calico pattern. Their eyes are green or yellow, with the occational rare blue.
In Arkanos, you are an outsider, but a welcome one, sure to not face discrimination unless it is from a wayward imperial zealot.

  • Way of the Bear: When you roll damage for an attack that used your strength modifier, you can treat the result of any die roll as 3.
  • Way of the Viper: When are wielding a spear or a quarterstaff, that weapon gains the finesse property, and its damage die is 1d8. If you wield it with two hands, it also gains the reach property, and its damage die becomes 1d10 instead.
   Languages: You can speak, read, and write Lhasa, a tonal and somewhat harsh language spoken almost exclusively by Yosri.

Other Ancestries

In addition to the proper races with defined ranges and cultures, the world of Dragonsea features a number of people born from unusual and perhaps forbidden unions. The human womb can incubate any seed, and so may bear a child by anything from a horse to a kraken, should a woman somehow find herself impregnated by such a creature. In fact, this is how the fiendspawn race first began. Occasionally, this can happen with a half-elf as well, and much more rarely with the other humanoid races. Furthermore, mortal children are sometimes born by immortal mothers, such as a dryad or a vampire who was impregnated by a mortal man.

Children with such unusual heritages often have trouble fitting into their native societies, and might even be thrown out of them entirely if they are deemed too disturbing to look at or interact with. Those with less obvious features of their nonhuman parents face less such issues, and are often accepted, or at least tolerated, as they are.

Traits

   Age: Your character's growth rate and lifespan depends on your parents, being roughly the same as your most short-lived humanoid parent.
   Size: Your size matches any of your humanoid parents.
   Speed: Your base walking speed is 30 feet if you're medium, or 25 if you're small.
   Proficiencies: You are proficient with any combination of three skills, artisan's tools, or weapons.
   Languages: You can speak, read, and write all languages spoken by both your parents, including special languages such as Orca.
   Ancestral Traits: You gain either:

  1. Two traits in any combination from any other race or subrace, but not one that alters your skills or ability scores.
  2. One trait from any other race or subrace as per the option above, and two from the Unusual Heritage Traits list below
  3. Three traits from the Unusual Heritage Traits list below.
This list of traits is extensive, but by no means comprehensive. You can make up almost any trait, but whether you can have it, and if it counts as two or three traits, is up to the DM. The Mutation Tables can be a good source of inspiration.
For instance, I once made a slug-man with a powerful prehensile radula by combining the "Chameleon-like tongue" (99) and "Lithophagic" (80) mutations from the Dr Drohlin's Minor Mutations table, and giving my character the ability to naturally attack with his radula in melee range for 1d4 + Strength modifier bludgeoning damage. The result being this natural weapon as my sole Ancestral Trait: "Sticky Radula: +(Strength modifier + proficiency bonus) to hit, reach 5ft, range 20ft, one target. Hit: 1d4+1d6+(Strength modifier) bludgeoning damage, and the target is grappled (escape DC11). A grappled target is pulled up to 20 feet towards me if it's medium or smaller, otherwise I am pulled up to 20ft towards it. This grapple ends if I attack another creature."

Unusual Heritage Traits

  • Adorable: You have advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks, but disadvantage on Charisma (Intimidation) checks, against creatures that can see you.
  • Alert to Danger: You can't be surprised while you are conscious. Creatures don't gain advantage on attack rolls against you as a result of being unseen by you.
  • Amphibious: You can breathe both air and water.
  • Armor Plating: Add 2 to your AC and subtract 5ft from your walking speed.
  • Awe Inspiring (Counts as 2 traits): You have advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) and Charisma (Intimidation) checks against creatures that can see you.
  • Bounding Leap: Your jump distance is tripled.
  • Burrowing: You gain a burrowing speed equal to half your movement speed.
  • Camouflage: You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks against being spotted.
  • Clawed/Taloned: Your unarmed strikes deal 1d4 + your Strength modifier slashing damage.
  • Clenching Jaws: Once per turn, as an attack, you may make an unarmed strike using your mouth. On a hit, you deal 1d6 + your Strength modifier piercing damage and lock onto the target with your jaws, grappling them.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60ft of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
  • Elemental Breath (Counts as 2 traits): When you gain this trait you choose a damage type, except for force or psychic, and an ability from Constitution, Wisdom, or Charisma. In place of an attack you may let loose a billowing breath of elemental energy. Each creature in a 15-foot cone must make a Dexterity saving throw (save DC 8 + your modifier for the Ability you chose + your proficiency bonus). A creature takes 2d6 plus your proficiency bonus damage of the chosen type on a failed save, and half as much on a successful one. After you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
  • Evasive Dash: After you take the Dash action, you have advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can see until the end of your next turn.
  • Fins/Flippers: You have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed.
  • Fleet-Footed: Your walking speed is increased by 10 feet.
  • Healing Touch (Counts as 2 traits): As an action you may touch a creature. The target regains 2d8 hit points. After you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.
  • Hurling Throw: As an action, you may throw a creature grappled by you a number of feet equal to 3 times your Strength modifier.
  • Hyper-Aware: You gain proficiency in the Wisdom (Perception) skill and double your proficiency bonus when determining your passive perception.
  • Imbue Weapon (Counts as 2 traits): When you gain this trait, choose two damage types, except force. As a bonus action you may channel your power into a weapon you are holding until the end of your turn. For the duration, the imbued weapon becomes magical, and when you hit a creature with an attack using the weapon, you deal an additional 2d6 damage of one of your chosen types. After you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.
  • Indomitable (Counts as 2 traits): You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, frightened, or stunned.
  • Keen Sight: You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.
  • Keen Senses: You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.
  • Legendary Hide (Counts as 2 traits): Choose a damage type, except for force, poison, or psychic. You have resistance to damage of that type.
  • Menacing: You gain proficiency in the Intimidation skill.
  • Nimble Squeeze: You have advantage on ability checks and saving throws to escape being grappled.
  • Pack Tactics (Counts as 2 traits): You have advantage on melee attack rolls against a creature if at least one of your allies is within 5ft of the creature and the ally isn't incapacitated.
  • Pouncing Leap: Your jump distance is doubled and the next attack you make after jumping at least 10 feet is made with advantage.
  • Prehensile Tail: You have a tail that can function as another hand. It is capable of holding items and grabbing things, but not manipulating things that require fine motor skills.
  • Pronounced Lineage (Counts as 0 traits): Choose a creature type other than construct or humanoid. You are considered to be that type as well as humanoid. The features of that creature type are obvious in your appearance, causing you to look quite strange and potentially deformed.
  • Protecting Aura (Counts as 2 traits): As a bonus action you may exude an aura of supernatural protection. Until the end of your next turn, all creatures of your choosing within 30 feet of you gain a +2 bonus to their AC. After you use this trait, you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.
  • Quiet Step: You have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks against being heard.
  • Quills: When a melee attack targeting you misses, you may deal piercing damage to the attacker equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • Rebirth (Counts as 3 traits): Once per long rest, if you have 0 hit points, at the beginning of your turn you may spend one or more of your Hit Dice and regain hit points equal to the total rolled, springing to your feet rather than making a death saving throw.
  • Regeneration: Any limbs you lose will slowly regrow over the course of 60 days.
  • Rush: If you move at least 20 feet towards a creature before hitting it with a melee attack, that creature must make a DC (8 + your Strength modifier + your proficiency bonus) Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
  • Sure-Footed: You have advantage on Strength and Dexterity saving throws against effects that would knock you prone.
  • Tentacled: Add 5ft to your melee attack range. You have advantage on checks made to grapple a creature.
  • Terrifying Outburst (Counts as 2 traits): When you gain this trait you choose an ability from Strength, Intelligence, and Charisma. As an action you may let loose a frightening manifestation of your will. Each creature of your choosing within 30 feet of you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (save DC 8 + your modifier for the Ability you chose + your proficiency bonus) or be frightened for 1 minute. A creature may repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success. After you use this trait you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.
  • Terrifying Presence: Any creature that makes a saving throw against an effect you create that could cause them to become frightened does so with disadvantage.
  • Thick Skin: Add 1 to your AC.
  • Tremorsense: You gain tremorsense out to a range of 60ft, letting you pinpoint the origin of vibrations in a substance you are in contact with within that range. Tremorsense can't be used to detect flying or incorporeal creatures.
  • Wall Climbing: You can climb difficult surfaces without needing to make an ability check.
  • Whirlwind of Power (Counts as 2 traits): As an action you may discorporate into a maelstrom of elemental energy until the end of your turn. Choose one of the following damage types: Acid, Cold, Fire, or Lightning. While discorporated you have a flying speed of 30ft and can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch without squeezing. At the end of your turn any creatures whose space you passed through while in this form must make a DC13 Dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 2d6 damage of the chosen type on a failed save, and half as much on a successful one. After you use this trait you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.
  • Wide Vision: You can see in all directions around you.
  • Winged (Counts as 3 traits): You have a flying speed of 50ft. You can't use this flying speed while you are wearing Medium or Heavy armor.
